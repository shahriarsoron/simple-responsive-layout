Hilsa Bhapa (Bangladesh)

Hilsa fish steamed in mustard paste — rich, oily, and considered the heart of Bangladeshi cuisine.

Price- About $8 to $20 per serving depending on season and quality of Hilsa fish.

Rendang (Indonesia)

Slow-cooked beef in a rich coconut and spice blend — deeply flavorful and legendary across Southeast Asia.

Price- Around $5 to $15 per serving in restaurants.

Nasi Lemak (Malaysia)

Fragrant coconut rice with spicy sambal, crispy anchovies, peanuts, and boiled egg — Malaysia’s ultimate comfort food.

Price- Typically $2 to $6 per plate at local stalls or casual eateries.

Bibimbap (South Korea)

A colorful bowl of rice topped with veggies, beef, fried egg, and spicy sauce — packed with flavor and texture.

Price- Typically $2 to $6 per plate at local stalls or casual eateries.

Sushi (Japan)

Classic vinegared rice with fresh fish or seafood — clean, elegant, and world-famous.

Price- Around $10 to $30 per serving at a decent restaurant.
